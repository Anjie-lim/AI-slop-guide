🛠️ The Stress-Test Prompt Formula

To build a high-yield prompt that intentionally exposes AI architecture weaknesses, raters must stack structural complexities using this rigorous 6-step blueprint.

1

Setting

Establish the baseline physical scene coordinates (e.g., a bustling night street market or a high-end jewelry store countertop).

2

Subjects

Enforce extreme density thresholds or high subject numbers (e.g., push explicitly for 15+ distinct people, items, or targets).

3

Actions

Introduce multiple, concurrent, and conflicting tasks performed by different entities to fracture prompt tracking.

4

Interactions

Force complex fine motor logic, hand positioning, weight distribution, or overlapping physical boundaries.

5

Complex Elements

Inject deliberate rendering trap vectors like mirror planes, legible typography strings, micro-textures, or grids.

6

Environment

Dictate deep perspective fields, hard lighting occlusion layers, and blocking elements to challenge foreground/background isolation.

💡 Operational Target

By assembling prompt components using this cascading complexity framework, you deny the model the ability to rely on generalized visual patterns, reliably prompting errors across texture, geometry, and tracking boundaries.

🔬 Quality Audit Workflows & Mnemonics

Core triage workflows and operational frameworks utilized by raters to systematically audit generations.

Step 1: Deep Zoom Audit

Micro-anomalies hide inside density fields. Never review an image at default layout scaling. Raters must zoom into focal clusters: eyes, hand boundaries, background layers, and text elements.

Step 2: Reality Contrast Check

Contrast all elements directly against biological and physical constraints. Raters must analyze each element by asking: "If this object or intersection existed in an unretouched scene, could it function logically?"

Step 3: Framework Triage

Apply the C-CARRP or BOUNDARIES mnemonics sequentially across the entire image space to catch elusive rendering errors.

Framework Alpha: The C-CARRP Checklist

Raters should use this core mental checklist for rapid asset verification loops:

CCount

Are object counts precise? Catch extra fingers, missing joints, or double limbs.

CConnect

Are structural joints correct? Watch for garments fused to skin or handles melting into palms.

AAlign

Are spatial tracking vectors accurate? Spot unaligned eyes or asymmetrical mirror angles.

RRepeat

Are repeated elements uniform? Identify duplicate clone faces or breaking grid lines.

RRead

Is typography coherent? Check strings for garbled letterforms or alien glyph arrays.

PPhysics

Are real-world physics maintained? Flag floating objects or broken shadow geometry.

Framework Beta: The B.O.U.N.D.A.R.I.E.S. Protocol

Mandated for deep inspections tracking organic details and material transitions:

B

U
Unnatural Textures: Flawed surface detail rendering, waxy artificial skin fields, or plastic skin textures.
N

D

A

R

I
Integrity: Core continuity. Isolate disjointed floaters, incomplete tools, or phantom assets lacking structural links.
E
Edges: Separation transitions. Blurry edges, melting visual limits, or smeared transition lines.
S
Spatial Relationships: Spatial plane consistency. Object clipping artifacts, floating models, or perspective tracking anomalies.

📋 The Rater Reporting Engine & Logging Standards

Standardized sequence rules and analytical reporting formats raters must follow when logging model vulnerabilities.

Mandated Spatial Sweep Sequence

To ensure thorough audit logs, raters must execute a methodical, top-to-bottom vertical scan of the frame:

  • 1 Faces & Eyes
  • 2 Hands & Fingers
  • 3 Arms & Legs
  • 4 Clothes & Badges
  • 5 Objects Being Held
  • 6 Text & Signage
  • 7 Mirrors & Glass
  • 8 Background Details
  • 9 Pattern Arrays
  • 10 Perspective Grids
